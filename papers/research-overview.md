# Research Overview: Alphalock, Deductive Games, and Reasoning Under Uncertainty

This repository references academic work exploring computational approaches to **Alphalock**, a constrained-feedback word game related to broader families of deductive games including **Wordle** and **Mastermind**.

The research investigates themes such as:

- reasoning under uncertainty
- exploration versus exploitation
- information gain
- entropy-based strategies
- reinforcement learning approaches to sequential decision making
- constrained feedback environments

Rather than reproducing the underlying research in detail, this repository focuses on discussing **high-level concepts** relevant to deductive word games and AI reasoning.

---

# Why Study Constrained-Feedback Games?

Games with incomplete information create environments where players or algorithms must:

- infer hidden states
- adapt strategies over time
- reduce uncertainty
- balance exploration and exploitation

Examples include:

- Wordle
- Mastermind
- Alphalock
- other deductive puzzle games

These environments can provide simplified settings for studying broader reasoning problems.

---

# Alphalock as a Distinct Environment

Compared with many Wordle-style games, Alphalock removes positional feedback.

Players receive only aggregate information about guesses.

This changes the nature of deduction and may encourage different solving strategies.

Questions that arise include:

- How should agents behave under constrained feedback?
- Which strategies reduce uncertainty most effectively?
- When should exploration transition toward exploitation?
- How do linguistic expectations influence decisions?
